# Shorter Timlin Wix Research Notes

## Expanded service text

- **Archaeological Research**  
  Historical and contextual investigation of places, sites, and artefacts tied to archival records. This includes tracing site history, historical maps, old place-name usage, property boundaries, and archival references to help confirm provenance and context.

- **Advising on Records**  
  Practical guidance on managing, preserving, and interpreting personal, family, estate, or institutional records. Typical support includes organizing archives, assessing significance, planning retention and digitization, and preparing records for long-term use.

- **Legal Research**  
  Document-based research to support legal and ownership-related enquiries. This usually means locating and interpreting historical records that provide evidence for disputes, inheritance tracing, land history, or institutional continuity.

- **Local and House History**  
  Narrative research into specific homes, buildings, and communities. It combines property records, occupant history, structural and ownership changes, and local documentation to create a coherent, defensible historical account.

- **Talks and Presentations**  
  Public or private sessions on archival practice, family and local history, and historical document interpretation. Useful for communities, institutions, and private clients who want an expert-led presentation or educational workshop.

- **Quote / Proposal Stage**  
  A dedicated intake step to define scope and produce a tailored estimate, including expected effort, timing, and deliverables for archival, transcription, or advisory work.
