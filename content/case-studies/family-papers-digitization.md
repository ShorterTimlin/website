## Challenge

The Morrison Family Trust held approximately 15 linear feet of family papers dating from 1847 to 1990, including correspondence, business records, photographs, and ephemera documenting five generations of a prominent mercantile family. The materials were stored in the family home's attic in deteriorating boxes and folders.

With family members now scattered across three continents, the Trust wanted to:
- Preserve deteriorating materials, especially fragile photographs and letters
- Create digital surrogates for family access
- Organize materials with proper archival arrangement
- Develop documentation explaining the collection's scope and significance
- Provide both physical and digital access to future generations

The collection included materials in fragile condition, mixed formats (letters, photographs, ledgers, newspapers), and required sensitive handling of private family information.

## Approach

We developed a comprehensive plan combining preservation, digitization, and description:

**Phase 1: Survey and Stabilization**
- Complete item-level survey documenting condition issues
- Emergency stabilization for at-risk materials
- Environmental monitoring recommendations

**Phase 2: Arrangement and Rehousing**
- Archival arrangement by family branch and generation
- Rehousing in archival folders and boxes
- Creation of detailed folder-level inventory

**Phase 3: Digitization**
- High-resolution scanning of all photographs (600 dpi)
- Document scanning for correspondence and papers (400 dpi)
- OCR processing for typed and printed materials
- Metadata creation with family member identification

**Phase 4: Access Systems**
- Digital asset organization and file naming
- Finding aid development
- Creation of digital access copies for family distribution

## Process

The survey phase revealed several items requiring immediate conservation attention, which we coordinated through a trusted conservator partner. We identified three major series:

1. Correspondence and Personal Papers (1847-1980)
2. Business Records - Morrison General Store (1872-1945)
3. Photographic Materials (1860s-1990)

During arrangement, we consulted with family members to identify individuals in photographs and provide context for business activities and family relationships. This collaborative approach enriched the metadata significantly.

Digitization followed archival processing, with the family trust retaining both the original materials (professionally rehoused) and receiving complete digital copies organized by series and folder.

We created rich metadata including:
- Date ranges and specific dates where identifiable
- Personal name authorities for family members
- Geographic locations
- Subject terms for business activities and family events
- Relationship documentation (family tree integration)

## Outcomes

**Deliverables:**
- 15 linear feet processed and rehoused in archival materials
- 2,847 photographs scanned at archival resolution
- 4,200+ pages of correspondence and documents digitized
- 156 business ledger pages digitized
- Comprehensive finding aid with item-level photo description
- Master digital files (TIFF) and access copies (JPEG/PDF)
- Organized file structure with consistent naming convention
- Digital preservation recommendations

**Impact:**
The Morrison family now has both preserved original materials and accessible digital copies. Family members around the world can view and share their heritage. The business records have proven valuable for local history research, with the family agreeing to make those materials available to researchers.

One family member wrote: "Seeing my great-great-grandparents' letters and photographs brought our family history to life. The organization and descriptions help us understand the context and connections between different branches of the family."

The digital collection has become a centerpiece of family gatherings, with different generations sharing stories prompted by the photographs and letters.
